About

I study how the resource-limited brain exploits structure in the environment to compute efficiently, which gives rise to systematic biases and variability in behavior. So far, my work has focused on single-trial decisions (risk, learning, multi-attribute valuation) through the lens of noisy/resource-rational inference.

Going forward, I’m extending the same formalism to sequential decision-making, planning, and learning, where structure resides in the temporal dependencies across trials.

Example (preprint)
Probability weighting arises from boundary repulsions of cognitive noise — probBoundaries

I’m also interested in modular hierarchical inference (stage-wise biases from perception to valuation), memory-limited updating (description–experience gap), and goal-dependent computations in multi-attribute choice.

Interests

  • Resource-rational cognition; noisy inference; efficient coding
  • Bayesian decoding; computational psychophysics; decision under risk/uncertainty
  • Sequential decisions, planning, and statistical/RL learning
  • Links to physics: stochastic processes, constraints, information & representation
