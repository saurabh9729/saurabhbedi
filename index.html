About

I study how the resource-limited brain exploits structure in the environment to compute efficiently, which gives rise to systematic biases and variability in behavior.

So far, my work has focused on single-trial decisions (risk, learning, multi-attribute valuation) through the lens of noisy/resource-rational inference.

Broadly, I work on: perception in risky decision-making and biases such as probability distortions in description; mechanisms behind the description–experience gap; decision biases in multi-attribute choice; and higher-order cognition as modular, resource-rational sequential inference.
